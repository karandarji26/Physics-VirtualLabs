Energy Gap of a Semiconductor

In semi-conductors at low temperatures, there are few charge carriers to move, so conductivity is quite low. However, with increase in temperature, a greater number of charge carriers get sufficient energy to be excited to the conduction band. This leads to increase in the number of free charge carriers and hence increase in conductivity.

When PN junction is placed in reverse bias, the current flows through the junction due to minority charge carriers only. The concentration of these charge carriers depends on band gap Eg. The saturation value, IS of reverse current depends on the temperature of junction diode and it is given by the following equation-

\[I_{s}=(nev_{e} + pev_{h})Ae^{-\frac{\text {E}g}{\text {k}T}}\]

Where,

    IS is reverse saturation current
    n is electron concentration on n side
    p is hole concentration on p side
    ve and vh are drift velocities of electrons and holes
    A is area of junction
    K is Boltzmann constant
    T is temperature
Taking log on both sides of the above equation we can write -
\[log_{e}I_{s}=log_{e}(nev_{e} + pev_{h})A {-\frac{\text {E}_{g}}{\text {k}T}}\] \[\therefore\ 2.303log_{10}I_{s}=2.303(log_{10}(nev_{e} + pev_{h})A) {-\frac{\text {E}_{g}}{\text {k}T}}\] \[\therefore\log_{10}I_{s}={\text CONSTANT} {-\frac{\text {E}_{g}}{\text 2.303{k}T}}\] \[\therefore\log_{10}I_{s}={\text CONSTANT} {-\frac{\text {E}_{g}}{\text 2.303 \times{8.625} \times10^{-5}T}}\] \[\therefore\log_{10}I_{s}={\text CONSTANT} {-\frac{\text {E}_{g}}{\text 2.303 \times{8.62} \times10^{-5}T}}\]
The reverse saturation current, IS and temperature of junction diode are related by equation - \[\therefore\log_{10}I_{s}=-5.04E_{g} \frac{10^{3}}{T} + CONSTANT\]
This equation is of type y = mx + c. So, if we plot a graph of
\[ log_{10}I_{s} \text { vs } \frac{10^{3}}{T}\]
, then slope of the graph is equal to -5.04 Eg and therefore we can write Energy gap
\[ E_{g} = \frac{slope}{5.04} \text{.}\]


PROCEDURE with Simulator :

  1. Switch on the circuit by clicking on the given button.
  2. Switch on the Thermometer to see temperature reading.
  3. Vary the reverse voltage using the given slider. You can note the reverse saturation current does not change with voltage.
  4. Select some reverse voltage and keep it constant during the experiment.
  5. Using the given slider, change the temperature in steps of 5℃ and note the temperature and corresponding current in the observation table.
  6. Plot a graph of
    \[ log_{10}I_{s} \text { vs } \frac{10^{3}}{T} \text{,}\]
    then slope of the graph is equal to -5.04 Eg and therefore we can write Energy gap
    \[ E_{g} = \frac{slope}{5.04} \text{.}\]
